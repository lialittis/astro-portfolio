## Schneier on Security
*Security news and analysis by Bruce Schneier*

### [Apple’s Camera Indicator Lights](https://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2026/03/apples-camera-indicator-lights.html) - March 30, 2026

<p>A <a href="https://daringfireball.net/2026/03/apple_enclaves_neo_camera_indicator">thoughtful review</a> of Apple&#8217;s system to alert users that the camera is on. It&#8217;s really well-designed, and important in a world where malware could surreptitiously start recording.</p>
<blockquote><p>The reason it&#8217;s tempting to think that a dedicated camera indicator light is more secure than an on-display indicator is the fact that hardware is generally more secure than software, because it&#8217;s harder to tamper with. With hardware, a dedicated hardware indicator light can be connected to the camera hardware such that if the camera is accessed, the light must turn on, with no way for software running on the device, no matter its privileges, to change that. With an indicator light that is rendered on the display, it&#8217;s not foolish to worry that malicious software, with sufficient privileges, could draw over the pixels on the display where the camera indicator is rendered, disguising that the camera is in use...</p></blockquote>
